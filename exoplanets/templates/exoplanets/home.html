What are exoplanets?

An exoplanet is any planet beyond our solar system. Most of them orbit other stars, but some free-floating exoplanets, called rogue planets, are untethered to any star. There are more than 5,600 confirmed exoplanets out of the billions that we believe exist.

Types of Exoplanets

Exoplanets are planets located outside our solar system, varying in size and composition. Types include hot Jupiters, super-Earths, and ice giants, each with unique characteristics and potential for supporting life.
